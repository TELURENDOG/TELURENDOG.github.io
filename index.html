Who am I? I'm nobody, just another curious soul wandering through an infinite universe of ideas. I build things, break things, fix things, and then somehow break them again five minutes later. Most of my time is spent arguing with computers, moving buttons two pixels to the left, then moving them back because apparently they were fine where they were.

While galaxies collide, stars are born, and black holes consume entire worlds, I'm usually busy trying to figure out why a piece of code worked perfectly yesterday and refuses to work today. Nobody knows why. Not even the code itself.

I've learned that every project starts with confidence, slowly turns into confusion, briefly becomes panic, and eventually transforms into something I'm strangely proud of. Somehow, after countless experiments, questionable late night decisions, and enough coffee to concern several medical professionals, this website came to life.

I'm not trying to change the world. I'm just here creating things, learning new stuff, making mistakes, and occasionally building something worth sharing. If anything on this website makes you smile, sparks your curiosity, or inspires you to create something of your own, then I'd say the mission was successful.

Anyway, welcome to my little corner of the cosmos.
